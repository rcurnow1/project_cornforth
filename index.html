ME




Over the past four years I have been training in Theatre and Performance at Plymouth Conservatoire. However, my theatrical career can be traced back to the age of eleven. I have spent eleven years engaging in theatrical training.

From this I have trained in a variety of disciplines. This makes it quite difficult to cater to a specific practice or profession. Instead, it would be easier to list of the fields I have trained in and titles that are appropriately associated with that.

Actor – Modernist and Post-Modernist Styles

Theatre Maker – Post Modernism

Performance Artist – physical endurance / durational styles that utilise the objective body as a site of resistance and critique.

Aerial Performer – Wall Pendulum and Counter weight Harnesses. (Basic Silk work too)

Autoethnographic and Practice as Researcher – Utilising my practices and autobiographical narratives to write new research into toxic masculinity, theatre and performance and training practices.

Physical Practice Developer – Mind/ Body dualism, Biomechanics, Self-awareness / reflexivity, strength, flexibility, aerobic development, endurance, duration, delicacy, nuance.
